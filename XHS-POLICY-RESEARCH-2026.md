## 1. Content Compliance Rules for Education / Study Abroad (留学) Accounts

### What's Allowed
- Sharing genuine personal study abroad experiences, campus life, application timelines
- Objective introductions of services and products through a certified Professional Account (专业号)
- Providing factual comparisons of programs, schools, and costs with verifiable data
- Educational content: study tips, portfolio preparation process, language test prep advice
- Using real case studies with proper attribution and no exaggeration

### What's Prohibited
- **Fabricating personas**: Pretending to be a "successful client," "senior student" (学姐/学长), or "study-abroad parent" (陪读宝妈) when you are actually an agent/institution
- **Fake UGC at scale**: Manufacturing user-generated content through matrix accounts to simulate organic word-of-mouth
- **Exaggerated claims**: "100% admission rate," "guaranteed Ivy League," "lowest price on the entire network"
- **Providing paid consultation without credentials**: Offering fee-based immigration or study abroad consulting without holding the relevant professional qualifications or even personal immigration status
- **Mixing immigration investment content into study abroad ads**: XHS classifies study abroad under "Education & Training -- Study Abroad" (教育培训-出国留学); ad materials must NOT include investment immigration content
- **Undisclosed commercial content**: All cooperative/sponsored notes must be declared through the Dandelion (蒲公英) platform
- **Health/efficacy-style absolute claims**: Phrases like "most effective," "number one," "best" are banned under China's Advertising Law as enforced on XHS

### Enforcement Data (H1 2025)
- 3.2 million fake notes removed
- 10,000 fake persona accounts penalized
- 600,000 low-quality AIGC notes intercepted
- 10 million+ black/grey-market accounts banned
- 16 study abroad accounts directly banned in one industry sweep (September 2025)

## 2. Marketing Regulations for 留学/作品集辅导 (Portfolio Guidance) Services

### Mandatory Requirements
- **Professional Account (专业号) certification** is required for any commercial activity. Select the correct industry category: "Education & Training -- Study Abroad" (教育培训-出国留学)
- **Transactions must occur on-platform**: Courses, materials, and consultations must be sold through 薯店 (XHS Shop). Private/off-platform transactions are explicitly prohibited since March 12, 2025
- **Real identity**: Must operate under a clear, verifiable professional identity -- no anonymous or disguised promotional accounts
- **Ad content review**: All promoted content goes through machine + human dual review. Sensitive terms trigger immediate scrutiny

### Prohibited Marketing Behaviors
- **Comparative flame-baiting**: "We crush competitor X" style content
- **Fake regular-user accounts** (素人号): Disguising institutional accounts as ordinary consumers
- **Ghostwriter Q&A schemes**: Coordinated fake questions and answers in comments
- **Price guarantee claims**: "Full refund if ineffective" or "guaranteed results"
- **Unverified future promises**: Cannot promise specific admission outcomes
- **Off-platform diversion**: No WeChat IDs, QR codes, external links, or watermarks in notes, comments, profiles, or live streams

### Portfolio Guidance Specific Notes
- Portfolio tutoring (作品集辅导) falls under education services; the same Professional Account and honest disclosure rules apply
- Pricing claims must be truthful; market rates for portfolio guidance are typically 80,000-200,000 RMB, and any pricing mentioned must be accurate
- Student work showcases must have proper consent and attribution; do not claim others' portfolios as your students' work

## 3. Account Naming Rules and Best Practices

### Technical Limits
- Maximum 12 Chinese characters (24 bytes/characters)
- Allowed special characters: `.` `·` `-` `()` `--` `《》` and standard Chinese/English symbols supported by registered trademarks/qualifications
- No duplicate names for Professional Accounts (first-come-first-served; malicious squatting prohibited)

### Prohibited in Names
- Imitating official accounts: "红书," "小红书官方," "小绿书," "小蓝书," or homophones like "小红叔," "小红薯," "XX薯"
- Commercial diversion: "XX淘宝店," "XX天猫" etc.
- Generic personal names: "李姐," "李老师," "小仙女"
- Title/position names: "XX董事长," "XX校长," "XX主任"
- Pinyin substitution for Chinese characters (e.g., "厨房Dao具")
- Any content violating laws, political sensitivity, public morals, or Advertising Law forbidden terms

### Best Practices for 留学 Accounts
- Include your specialty or niche: e.g., "艺术留学Lab" or "设计作品集工作室"
- Keep it memorable, searchable, and professional
- Avoid overly generic names that could be confused with personal diaries
- If operating as a business, the name should clearly reflect the service nature

## 4. Hashtag / Tag Usage Limits and Rules

### Limits
- **Maximum 10 tags per note**
- Tags can include: topic hashtags (#话题), custom topics, location/address, brand tags, language tags, product names

### Rules and Best Practices
- **Relevance over volume**: Do not stuff unrelated trending hashtags; the algorithm penalizes irrelevance
- **Participate in official topics**: Joining 2+ official topic activities per week earns extra exposure weighting
- **Don't modify tags on viral notes immediately**: Wait before editing tags on notes that are gaining traction, as changes can disrupt distribution
- **Mix broad and niche tags**: Combine high-volume tags (e.g., #留学 #艺术留学) with specific long-tail tags (e.g., #英国皇艺申请 #作品集排版)
- **Keyword integration**: Tags are part of XHS's search/SEO system; align them with the keywords you want to rank for

## 5. Cross-Account Promotion (矩阵运营) Policies

### Current Enforcement Status (2025-2026)
XHS has been conducting **routine crackdowns on matrix operations** since September 2025 across seven major industry categories including education. This is now a permanent, ongoing enforcement effort -- not a one-time campaign.

### What Triggers Penalties
- **Same device/IP registration**: Registering 3+ accounts from the same device or IP address triggers automatic flags
- **Content duplication**: Copying the same or near-identical content across multiple accounts
- **Cross-platform content transplant**: Taking content from Douyin/WeChat and reposting on XHS without substantial modification
- **Coordinated comment/engagement schemes**: Multiple accounts liking, commenting on, or redirecting to each other
- **Batch account behavior patterns**: Similar posting times, content formats, and engagement patterns across accounts
- **Undisclosed commercial relationships** between accounts in a matrix

### Penalties
- New accounts with abnormal behavior: **immediate ban**
- Old accounts triggering matrix flags: **7-30 day function restrictions**
- Severe cases: **permanent ban** (one operator lost 37 accounts overnight, including accounts still in "cultivation" phase)
- Banned accounts' associated devices/IPs may be flagged, making new account creation difficult

### Compliant Matrix Operation (What's Still Possible)
- Each account must have **genuinely differentiated content and positioning**
- Use **different devices, different IPs, different phone numbers**
- Avoid cross-promotion patterns; each account should function independently
- The new paradigm is "quality over quantity" -- 2-3 well-maintained accounts beat 20 low-effort clones
- KOS (Key Opinion Sales) employee accounts now require **5,000+ followers** on the personal account before binding as an employee account

## 6. 薯条 (Promoted Posts) and 聚光 (Search Ads) Rules

### Tool Overview
| Tool | Purpose | Min Spend | Best For |
|------|---------|-----------|----------|
| **薯条** | Lightweight content boosting | 75 RMB | Testing note performance, small-budget amplification |
| **聚光** | Full ad platform (feed + search) | Higher threshold | Lead generation, search ranking, conversion campaigns |
| **蒲公英** | Influencer collaboration | Varies | KOL/KOC partnerships |

### New/Low-Weight Account Considerations
- **Cold-start strategy**: Use 薯条 for small-amount testing (50-500 RMB) to validate note click-through rates before scaling
- **Optimal 薯条 timing**: Weekday 8-10 AM / Weekend 8-11 PM
- **2026 update**: Mobile 聚光Lite is being discontinued; a new version of 薯条 will replace it as the primary mobile ad tool
- Paid traffic now **outweighs organic traffic** in XHS's ecosystem as of 2025; the platform has entered a "paid triggers organic" phase
- For new accounts: focus on building 10-20 quality organic notes before investing heavily in paid promotion
- **KOS binding requirement**: Personal accounts need 5,000+ followers to bind as employee accounts for ad purposes

### Ad Content Review for Education
- Study abroad services fall under regulated advertising categories
- No absolute efficacy claims in ad copy
- No guaranteed outcome promises (admission, visa approval, etc.)
- Must match the certified Professional Account's industry category
- Ad materials cannot contain immigration investment content

## 7. Posting Frequency Recommendations for Account Recovery (权重修复)

### If Account Is Throttled/Penalized
1. **Immediate audit (Day 1)**: Review and delete/edit all notes containing exaggerated claims, fake cases, or policy-violating content. Take screenshots of edits for appeal purposes within 24 hours
2. **Recovery posting (Days 1-3)**: Publish 2-3 high-quality original notes to signal account health to the algorithm
3. **Stabilization (Weeks 1-4)**: Maintain consistent posting at 3-5 notes per week; do not go silent
4. **Weekend consistency**: Accounts that maintain weekend updates see Monday traffic recovery 3x faster than those that stop abruptly

### General Posting Frequency Guidelines
| Account Stage | Recommended Frequency |
|---------------|----------------------|
| New account (first month) | 1 per day or 3-5 per week |
| Established account | 3-5 per week, stable rhythm |
| Recovery period | 2-3 quality posts in first 3 days, then stabilize at 3-5/week |
| Dormant account reactivation | Start with 2-3 casual/lifestyle posts to "warm up," then resume professional content |

### New Account Cultivation Period
- New accounts: cultivate for **2-4 weeks** before expecting traction
- Old/previously-penalized accounts: **1-2 months** rehabilitation period
- The first 2 hours after publishing are critical: actively engage with comments during this window to signal content quality to the algorithm

## 8. 留学移民行业不规范营销治理公告 -- Enforcement Details

### Background
XHS published a dedicated governance announcement targeting irregular marketing in the study abroad and immigration industry. This was part of a broader September 2025 crackdown affecting seven industry verticals.

### Specific Violations Targeted
1. **Fabricated identities**: Accounts pretending to be successful immigration clients, study abroad alumni (学姐/学长), or accompanying parents (陪读宝妈/宝爸) when they are actually agents
2. **Batch fake UGC**: Using account matrices to manufacture fake user testimonials at scale
3. **Unlicensed consulting**: Charging for immigration/study abroad consultation without proper qualifications, or without even holding immigration status themselves
4. **Persona packaging**: Elaborate fake backstories to build false trust
5. **Mixing regulated services**: Blending investment immigration content into study abroad advertising

### Required Compliance
- **Professional Account certification is mandatory**: Must operate under a clear, verified professional identity in the community
- **Objective and truthful service descriptions**: No exaggeration of outcomes or fabrication of case studies
- **Proper industry classification**: Select the correct sub-category during Professional Account setup
- Content previously published that violates these rules should be **retroactively cleaned up** to avoid ongoing penalties

### Penalty Structure (Escalating)
1. Content throttling (限流) -- reduced distribution
2. Content removal (删除笔记)
3. Temporary mute (禁言) -- 24 hours to 30 days
4. Temporary account suspension
5. **Permanent ban** for severe or repeated violations

## 9. Best Posting Times for Art/Design Niche on Chinese Social Media

### General XHS High-Traffic Windows
| Time Slot | Window | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Morning commute | 7:00-9:00 AM | Good for quick-read content |
| Lunch break | 12:00-2:00 PM | Extended browsing session |
| Evening commute | 6:00-8:00 PM | High volume but high competition |
| Pre-sleep | 10:00 PM-12:00 AM | Longest browsing sessions |

### Art/Design/Education Niche Specifically
- **Knowledge/educational content** performs best at **8:30-9:30 PM** (after-work learning window for young professionals -- your core audience for 作品集辅导)
- **Tuesday/Thursday 11:30 AM-12:00 PM**: Decision-makers browse during meeting breaks
- **Weekday 8-10 AM**: Good for 薯条 boosted content
- **Weekend 8-11 PM**: Peak engagement for weekend learners

### Critical Insight for Small Accounts (<10K followers)
Accounts under 10,000 followers receive **only 1/3 the exposure during peak hours** compared to off-peak times, because they are drowned out by larger accounts. Strategy for smaller accounts:
- **Post 30-60 minutes BEFORE peak windows** (e.g., 6:30 AM, 11:30 AM, 9:30 PM) to enter the recommendation pool before the flood
- Or target **off-peak windows** where competition is lower

### Post-Publication Engagement
- The **first 2 hours** after posting determine algorithmic distribution
- Actively reply to every comment during this window
- Encourage saves (收藏) and shares -- these weigh more than likes in XHS's algorithm

## 10. AI Content Generation -- XHS Labeling Requirements

### Legal Framework
China's "Methods for Labeling AI-Generated and Synthetic Content" (人工智能生成合成内容标识办法) took effect **September 1, 2025**, issued jointly by CAC, MIIT, MPS, and NRTA. This is a national standard, not just a platform rule.

### XHS-Specific Requirements (Community Guidelines 2.0, January 19, 2026)
- **Mandatory declaration**: If any part of a note (text, images, video) is AI-generated or AI-assisted, creators **must proactively label it**
- **How to declare**: Publish Note > Settings > Content Type Declaration > check "AI-assisted creation"
- **Scope**: Applies regardless of the proportion of AI content; even partial use requires labeling
- **Applies to originals and reposts**: Reshared content containing AI elements must also be labeled

### Prohibited AI Uses on XHS
- Impersonation of real people
- Military/police image tampering
- Creating fake personas
- Generating false information or fake news
- Producing vulgar/explicit content
- Manufacturing deceptive engagement (fake comments, reviews)
- AI-driven mass marketing content

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Reduced recommendations (限流)
- Restricted search visibility
- Reduced exposure
- Content removal
- Account ban for repeated violations

### Platform Enforcement Scale
- H1 2025: **600,000 low-quality AIGC notes intercepted**
- H1 2025: **10 million+ black/grey-market accounts banned** (many involved in automated AI content farms)

### Practical Guidance
- Using AI to brainstorm, outline, or polish drafts is acceptable -- but **label it**
- AI-generated images (Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.) must be declared
- Adding personal insights, real photos, and genuine experience to AI-assisted content improves both compliance and algorithmic performance
- The platform's stance: AI is a tool, not a replacement for authentic sharing

## Key Takeaways for a 留学/作品集辅导 Operation

1. **Get certified immediately**: Professional Account with correct "Education & Training -- Study Abroad" category is non-negotiable
2. **Sell through 薯店 only**: All transactions on-platform; no WeChat/external diversion
3. **Real identity, real cases**: Only share genuine student outcomes with permission; never fabricate personas
4. **Label AI content**: If you use AI for any part of content creation, declare it
5. **Quality over quantity**: 3-5 well-crafted notes per week beats daily low-effort posts
6. **Matrix operations are high-risk**: If running multiple accounts, they must be genuinely differentiated with separate devices/IPs
7. **Invest in paid promotion wisely**: Start with small 薯条 tests, scale what works, ensure ad copy passes review
8. **Post strategically**: Target 8:30-9:30 PM for educational content; engage actively in the first 2 hours
9. **Audit existing content now**: Remove anything with exaggerated claims, fake testimonials, or undisclosed commercial intent
10. **Stay current**: XHS updates rules frequently; monitor official announcements monthly
